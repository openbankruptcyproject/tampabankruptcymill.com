Costs, Timeline, and What to Know

Chapter 7 Basics for Tampa Filers

Chapter 7 bankruptcy provides a relatively quick discharge of most unsecured debts - credit cards, medical bills, personal loans - typically within 3 to 4 months of filing. Unlike Chapter 13, there is no multi-year repayment plan. The trade-off is that non-exempt assets may be liquidated, though most consumer filers retain all of their property under applicable exemptions.

Florida's unlimited homestead exemption makes Chapter 7 an attractive option for homeowners. Chapter 7 discharge rates in the Middle District of Florida are consistently strong. The state's lack of income tax means that the means test relies more heavily on actual expense calculations.

Key advantage over Chapter 13: Chapter 7 discharge rates typically exceed 90%. Chapter 13 completion rates are often below 50%. For qualifying filers, Chapter 7 is the faster, cheaper, and far less risky option.

What Chapter 7 Costs in Tampa

The costs of filing Chapter 7 in the Middle District of Florida (M.D. Fla.) include:

Court filing fee: $338

Set by the Judicial Conference. Can be paid in installments or waived for qualifying low-income filers.

Attorney fees: $1,000 - $2,500

Varies by attorney and case complexity. Paid upfront before filing. Get quotes from multiple attorneys and compare.

Credit counseling: $30 - $100

Two required courses (pre-filing and pre-discharge). Many providers offer online courses at the lower end of this range.

Total cost for a typical Tampa Chapter 7 case: $1,400 to $2,900. This is significantly less than the total cost of a Chapter 13 case, which includes higher attorney fees plus years of plan payments.

The Tampa Chapter 7 Timeline

A typical Chapter 7 case in Tampa follows this timeline:

Day 0: Filing

Petition filed with the M.D. Fla.. Automatic stay takes effect immediately, stopping most collection activity.

Days 21-50: 341 Meeting

Meeting of creditors conducted by the Chapter 7 trustee. Typically lasts 5-10 minutes for straightforward consumer cases.

Day 60 after 341: Discharge

If no objections are filed, the discharge order is entered approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting.

Total: 3-4 months

From filing to discharge, most straightforward Chapter 7 cases close within 3 to 4 months.

Do You Qualify? The Means Test

Chapter 7 eligibility is determined by the means test under 11 U.S.C. § 707(b). The test compares your household income to the Florida state median for your family size.

Below median income: You generally qualify for Chapter 7 automatically.

Above median income: A detailed calculation of your disposable income determines whether you can file Chapter 7 or must file Chapter 13 instead.

Beware of steering. Some attorneys recommend Chapter 13 even when the client qualifies for Chapter 7, because Chapter 13 attorney fees are higher and paid through the plan. Always ask whether you qualify for Chapter 7 and, if so, why Chapter 13 is being recommended instead.

Mill Warning Signs in Chapter 7

While bankruptcy mills primarily profit from Chapter 13, Chapter 7 is not immune from quality issues. Watch for these signs in Tampa:

Steering toward Chapter 13. If you qualify for Chapter 7 but your attorney recommends Chapter 13 without a clear, case-specific explanation, that is a red flag.

Template petitions. Your bankruptcy petition should reflect your actual financial situation in detail. If schedules appear to use round numbers or generic expense categories, the petition may not be adequately individualized.

No discussion of exemptions. Florida exemption planning is critical to protecting your assets in Chapter 7. An attorney who does not discuss exemptions in detail may not be providing adequate representation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Chapter 7 bankruptcy cost in Tampa?
The court filing fee for Chapter 7 is $338. Attorney fees in Tampa typically range from $1,000 to $2,500, paid upfront before filing. Total cost including credit counseling ($15-50 for each of two required courses) is typically $1,400 to $2,900.
What is the Chapter 7 discharge rate in Tampa?
Chapter 7 discharge rates are generally above 90% across most federal bankruptcy courts, including the M.D. Fla.. Less than 1% of Chapter 7 cases are typically dismissed. This makes Chapter 7 significantly less risky than Chapter 13 for qualifying filers.
How long does Chapter 7 take in Tampa?
A typical Chapter 7 case takes 3 to 4 months from filing to discharge. The 341 meeting of creditors is held 21-50 days after filing, and the discharge is typically entered about 60 days after the 341 meeting, assuming no objections.
Do I qualify for Chapter 7 in Tampa?
Eligibility depends on the means test under 11 U.S.C. § 707(b). If your household income is below the Florida state median for your family size, you generally qualify. If above median, a detailed calculation of your disposable income determines eligibility. Check your eligibility at meanstest.org.
Can I file Chapter 7 if I already filed bankruptcy before?
You can file Chapter 7 at any time, but you cannot receive a discharge if you received a Chapter 7 discharge within the last 8 years or a Chapter 13 discharge within the last 6 years (11 U.S.C. § 727(a)(8)-(9)). Check your eligibility at 1328f.com.
